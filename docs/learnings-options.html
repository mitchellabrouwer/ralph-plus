A

Capped LEARNINGS.md with Rolling Window

Keep the current single-file approach but cap it. When archiving, only keep the most recent N entries (e.g. 10). Oldest get dropped. Simple, zero new infrastructure. The tradeoff is that older insights vanish permanently.

Zero new files Predictable context size Loses old insights No human capture path
B

Two-Tier: LEARNINGS.md (hot) + auto-memory (cold)

LEARNINGS.md stays capped (recent task patterns for agents). But on archive, older entries get promoted to Claude auto-memory files organized by topic (e.g. testing.md, bash.md). Auto-memory is always in your conversation context, so nothing is truly lost, and topic files let you grep for specific things. Agents get the hot file; you get the full picture.

Nothing lost Agents stay lean Searchable by topic Moderate complexity Auto-memory has 200 line MEMORY.md limit
C

Curated Playbook (Human-in-the-Loop)

After each task completes, the committer proposes learnings but a human (you) decides what actually makes it into a curated PLAYBOOK.md. Think of it like a team's runbook: only battle-tested patterns survive. Keeps signal high, noise low. The tradeoff is it requires your time and attention.

Highest signal-to-noise You control what matters Requires your time Can fall behind if you skip reviews
D

Hybrid: Auto-Curated + Human Escalation

Combine B and C. The archive step auto-categorizes learnings into topic files in auto-memory (cold storage). LEARNINGS.md stays as a rolling window for agents. But you also get a notes.md scratchpad where you can jot things down anytime, and a periodic "review" where Claude surfaces the top unvetted learnings for you to promote or discard.

Nothing lost Agents stay lean Human + human capture paths Periodic curation Most moving parts
